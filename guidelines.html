SDS Billing Profile Guidelines

This document outlines the standards, policies, and procedures governing Billing Profiles within the SDS platform. All team members and partners must adhere to these guidelines when creating or modifying billing records.

1. Overview

A Billing Profile is a named configuration that captures all the information needed to generate an accurate invoice for a customer or internal cost center. Profiles are used across SDS modules to ensure consistent, auditable billing.

1.1 Scope

These guidelines apply to:

  • All customer-facing billing profiles
  • Internal cost-center charge-back profiles
  • Partner and reseller billing arrangements

1.2 Key Definitions

Term Definition
Billing Profile A named set of billing parameters associated with an account or cost center.
Invoice Cycle The periodic interval (monthly, quarterly, annual) at which invoices are generated.
Payment Term The agreed period within which payment must be received after an invoice is issued (e.g., Net 30).
Tax Code The jurisdiction-specific identifier used to calculate applicable taxes.
GL Account The General Ledger account code to which revenue or cost is posted.

2. Profile Creation Requirements

Every billing profile must include the mandatory fields listed below before it can be activated in the system.

2.1 Mandatory Fields

  • Profile Name – Unique, human-readable identifier (max 80 chars)
  • Account ID – System-assigned customer or cost-center identifier
  • Currency – ISO 4217 code (e.g., USD, EUR, GBP)
  • Invoice Cycle – Monthly, Quarterly, or Annual
  • Payment Term – Net 15, Net 30, Net 60, or custom
  • Billing Contact Email – Must be a verified corporate address
  • GL Account – Must exist in the active chart of accounts

2.2 Optional Fields

  • Purchase Order (PO) Number
  • Tax Exemption Certificate
  • Secondary Billing Contact
  • Custom Invoice Notes (max 500 chars)

3. Approval Workflow

Profile changes follow a two-stage approval process to maintain data integrity and comply with financial controls.

3.1 Stage 1 – Submitter Review

  1. Submitter completes all mandatory fields.
  2. System performs automated validation (format checks, GL account lookup).
  3. Submitter clicks Submit for Approval.

3.2 Stage 2 – Finance Approval

  1. Finance team receives email notification.
  2. Reviewer verifies tax codes, GL mapping, and payment terms.
  3. Reviewer approves or rejects with comments within 2 business days.
⚠️ Note: Profiles in Pending Approval status cannot be used to generate invoices until fully approved.

4. Naming Conventions

Profile names must follow the pattern below to ensure uniqueness and readability:

[Region]-[AccountType]-[AccountID]-[YYYY]

5. Tax & Compliance

5.1 Tax Code Assignment

Tax codes are automatically suggested based on the account's billing address jurisdiction. Reviewers must confirm or override suggestions when:

  • The customer holds a valid tax-exemption certificate.
  • The account crosses multiple tax jurisdictions.
  • The service category requires a special tax treatment.

5.2 Exemptions

To apply a tax exemption, attach the certificate in PDF format (max 5 MB) before submission. Exemptions expire on the certificate's stated date and must be renewed by the billing contact.

6. Modification & Archival Policy

6.1 Allowed Modifications Post-Activation

The following fields may be updated on an active profile without requiring full re-approval:

  • Billing Contact Email
  • Secondary Billing Contact
  • Custom Invoice Notes
  • PO Number

Changes to Currency, Invoice Cycle, Payment Term, or GL Account require a new approval cycle and take effect on the next invoice date.

6.2 Archival

Profiles are automatically archived when:

  • The associated account is closed.
  • The profile has been inactive (no invoices generated) for 12 consecutive months.

Archived profiles are retained for 7 years for audit purposes and cannot be reactivated without Finance approval.
